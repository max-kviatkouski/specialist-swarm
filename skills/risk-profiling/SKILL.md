# Risk Profiling

> FICTIONAL demo content for a hackathon. Not investment advice.

Risk has **two independent dimensions**. Score each 0–100, then assign a band.

## 1. Capacity (ability to take risk) — objective

Raise the score for: long time horizon, stable/high income, large emergency reserve,
low fixed obligations, surplus assets beyond goals. Lower it for: short horizon, single
income, large near-term liabilities, thin reserves.

| Capacity score | Reading |
| --- | --- |
| 0–35 | Low — needs the money soon or income is fragile |
| 36–65 | Moderate |
| 66–100 | High — long horizon, secure income, surplus |

## 2. Tolerance (willingness to take risk) — behavioral

From stated attitude and past behavior. **A client who sold into a past selloff has lower
revealed tolerance than they claim.** Weight revealed behavior over stated preference.

## Assigning the band

Map the **lower** of capacity/tolerance to the band — never recommend more risk than the
client can stomach OR afford:

| Lower score | Band |
| --- | --- |
| 0–25 | Conservative |
| 26–45 | Moderate |
| 46–65 | Moderate Growth |
| 66–82 | Growth |
| 83–100 | Aggressive |

## Reconciling a mismatch (capacity ≫ tolerance)

This is common (high earner, nervous investor). Resolution: anchor to the **lower (tolerance)**
band, and reduce risk through **diversification** (cut single-stock concentration, add bonds/intl)
rather than cutting the overall equity weight to cash. Document the rationale.

## Output expectations

Return: capacityScore, toleranceScore, the assigned band, and a 1–2 sentence rationale that
names the dominant driver. Flag any mismatch explicitly for the Compliance Reviewer.
